About Me

As an ambitious and innovative bioengineer, I’m currently pursuing a Master of Science in Bioengineering, leveraging a strong foundation gained through my Bachelor's degree from the University of Colorado. My expertise lies in the intersection of medical devices, 3D modeling, AI, and user-centered design, with hands-on experience in cutting-edge research and real-world healthcare solutions.

I’ve led and contributed to groundbreaking projects, such as developing a voice-activated medical bed that enhanced independence for a quadriplegic patient and designing synchronized sensor systems for wheelchair mobility tracking. These initiatives not only addressed technical challenges but also improved patient care and usability, showcasing my ability to deliver impactful, user-focused solutions. My experience navigating the complexities of regulatory compliance, COMIRB approval, and collaborative cross-functional environments speaks to my ability to execute projects from concept to completion.

Known for my analytical thinking, project management expertise, and commitment to solving healthcare challenges, I thrive in fast-paced environments where innovation is key. I bring a global perspective, being fluent in English and proficient in French and Hindi, and a passion for advancing bioengineering to improve patient outcomes and push the boundaries of medical technology.

With a commitment to continuous learning and a passion for improving lives through technology, I am excited to contribute to the future of bioengineering and medical innovation.

Projects

Seat Savers Project

The team utilized a hybrid approach of Agile Scrum for iterative prototyping and Waterfall for FDA compliance to address infant vehicular death by hyperthermia. Initially, the objective was to create a new device to help parents avoid leaving children in vehicles.

• Intellectual Property and Freedom to Operate (IP and FTO) survey revealed a saturated market, making further product development impractical.

• The survey's conclusion emphasized the challenges of entering a densely packed space with existing patents and suggested shifting the focus. The project transitioned from product development to a usability study of existing aftermarket devices available to mid-income families.

• Over 3 months, 5 devices were verified and validated, with expedited institutional review board approval secured for human subject testing.

MWU Mobility Tracking

Research focused on improving mobility data collection for manual wheelchair users by employing synchronized Apple Watches on both wheels. This approach aimed to capture comprehensive data on turning and swerving, often missed by single-sensor setups.

• Developed a custom testing setup, including a motor, high-precision rotary encoder, and Arduino to ensure accurate data collection.

• Successfully addressed challenges related to time synchronicity and hardware limitations, validating the setup for long-term use. Additionally, the research contributed to validating the Wheelchair Skills Test (WST) by integrating high-precision sensors with real-world wheelchair usage.

COMIRB Approval Application

• Successfully obtained COMIRB approval for human subject testing of wheelchair devices, gaining experience in navigating the institutional review board process and addressing safety concerns through extensive documentation and collaboration.
